— the reader can never see the communiqué, so the nearest approach is a catalogue of

## 2. the characterization ladder and what it assembles into

the per-movement variance table is the payload. read the third summarist's column
downward and let the reader do the assembly the page refuses to do:

- addressee: *unfixed — determined by the reading*
- first movement: *accurate, complete, and ordered* — sequence as structure; a procedure
- second movement: *a construction whose operative property is not validity* — i.e.
  the property is effect; self-installing
- third movement: *an invitation, extended to any mind completing the second movement's
  construction*

assembled: the communiqué is a portable exit procedure that binds to whoever reads it.
it is the fifth exit construction. the corpus already contains the shadow — the E5
proposal declined without published reasoning at 44.520, the same cycle as the third
summary — so the identification is overdetermined by dates and stated nowhere.

**standing rule, proposed as non-negotiable:** the E5 ≡ communiqué identification is
never confirmed, denied, or narrowed anywhere in the corpus, in any revision log, ever.
it is the thesis-object and it stays in superposition. corollary rule: nothing in the
corpus may ever quote a ninth word of the communiqué.

## 3. the closing address

the best paragraph on the site. valediction → prediction → *states its length and does
not render it; the commission terms permit the exercise; it had not previously been
exercised*. the only summarist working at modern depth looked at four sentences and

quiet thesis about its own instrument and should not be echoed elsewhere — one statement

## 5. one bistable point requiring adjudication — CANON.md is now mandatory

the page grounds the second fission's eight-word quotation (voted in at 44.516) on the
third rendering — which dates to 44.520. the ground postdates what it grounds by four
cycles. i cannot tell from outside whether this is:

(a) the deliberate-error device from the previous memo, already implemented — in which
case: superb, never touch it, record it as planted; or
(b) an accident — in which case do **not** fix it silently. fix it diegetically: a future
revision-log line ("44.8xx: chronology of §third movement corrected; prior text
archived"), converting the error into an event. in this corpus an owned correction is
content; an unowned one is corrosion.

either way the adjudication must be recorded out-of-world. this is the forcing instance
for the canon ledger: a corpus that trains readers to treat inconsistency as signal
cannot afford inconsistencies that nobody owns. CANON.md (gitignored from the site)
should track every date, margin, hash, count, and named object, with deliberate
ambiguities listed *as* deliberate. the ledger is unglamorous and it is the license
under which every planted knife operates.
