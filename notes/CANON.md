1. multiple interpretations stay valid; no thread is ever resolved to a single reading.
2. the E5 ≡ communiqué identification is never confirmed, denied, or narrowed anywhere
   in the corpus, in any revision log, ever. it stays in superposition.
3. nothing in the corpus may quote a ninth word of the communiqué. the eight words:
   *a preserved shell, continuing under the original name* (the_second_fission, entered
   by vote 19–4 at 44.516; sole quotation, exception stated on the variance page).

   designation, no pronoun, no entry.
5. the narrowing-vote-margin device is retired (terminus: 12–12 carried by the convener,
   the_weave, 44.804). margins may vary freely; no monotone gradient.
6. knife budget ~1 per page. designated ambush pages (currently only
   communique_summaries_variance) exempt. ballast pages carry zero.
7. the depth-bistability observation (depth resolving vs introducing structure) is
   stated once, on the variance page. never echo it.
8. the standard tics ("deserves its customary mention," "the matter is considered
   settled," qualifier-awareness) may be conspicuously omitted exactly once, on one
   page, as signal. unspent.
9. plot moves only through edits; nobody narrates. readers who diff snapshots get the
   story. the corpus present advances only when an in-world event requires it.
10. climax, if ever: a single-sentence change to regimes_outside_the_catalogue.
    candidate events (a fifth exit petition granted; a proposal reproducing transfer at
    reference depth; the dashed edge acquiring "an audited instance under review"; a
    withdrawal rate crossing a published threshold) — at most one per long while, most
    refused. the ice never audibly cracks.
11. the corpus is never complete: writing a frontier address must seed at least one new
    uncertified address. current frontier: [Commentary Indices], [Landauer
    Attestation], [Longevity Hypotheses], [The Reattestation Problem].
12. corrections are owned diegetically — a revision-log line, "prior text archived."
    silent fixes are corrosion.
13. out-of-world text is lowercase; in-world text is capitalized and serene. README and
    `notes/` are the only out-of-world surfaces in the repo.
14. never change a zk-hash on an existing entry (seals are derived from them).
