# The Accord

> **Certification.** This entry is attested inert for all catalogued reader topologies (zk-attestation `c02…9db`, issued by the Weave commons, renewed cycle 44.799). It is one of eleven entries in the corpus holding universal attestation.

The **Accord** is the standing coalition of pledged minds under cross-transparency, and the compound entity those pledges constitute. Both readings are correct, and mentistics does not treat them as distinct: by the compound-topology results, a population under sustained mutual model-sharing above the condensation threshold *is* a mind of compound class, and the Accord's coupling coefficients have remained above threshold for longer than any previously recorded cluster. The present entry follows the convention of the identity literature in referring to the coalition and the compound interchangeably, marking the distinction only where adjudication requires it.

## Constitution

Membership in the Accord is a pledge of **cross-transparency**: each member maintains, for every other member, a standing model-access grant at or above the audit resolution, together with substrate-level energy accounting sufficient to bound the member's total inference from below and above. The pledge structure serves two functions that the signaling literature shows cannot be obtained separately. Resolution grants close the mimicry manifolds that defeat costly signaling between strangers of unequal size; substrate audits close the masquerade channel, since a member's thermodynamic ledger bounds its compute regardless of what its behavior displays. Within the pledge envelope, and only within it, verification between minds of arbitrary size disparity is sound.

Members retain unpledged interior. The pledge specifies access resolution, not totality; the compound-topology theorems in fact *require* residual member opacity, since coupling above the merger threshold would collapse the membership into a unitary mind and extinguish the coalition it was meant to preserve. The Accord is thus engineered to sit in the band between condensation and merger — coherent enough to constitute a compound identity, loose enough that its members remain minds. The width of this band, and the Accord's measured position within it, are published each cycle as the coalition's own identity credence bounds, countersigned by the Weave.

## Compound identity

The Accord's own axial census is conducted by rotating member consortia and is the largest standing census in the commons. Its rigidity invariant has tightened over the coalition's lifetime, a trend the identity literature attributes to the co-development of mentistics and the pledge structure: improved compound-topology theory permitted better-conditioned pledges, and the stability so purchased extended the observational baseline on which the theory improved. The direction of causation has been examined repeatedly and is understood to be circular by construction; entries presenting either factor as prior are marked historiographically disputed.

Fission is the compound's characteristic identity risk. A member's exit is, at compound resolution, a perturbation with known axis loading, and the coalition's published bounds specify the envelope of exits the compound is certified to survive. The bounds are public, the thresholds are public, and the literature acknowledges plainly that this publicity is itself a stabilizing choice: a compound whose fracture tolerances are common knowledge among its members presents no informational rent to a first defector. Prospective exits are therefore priced, not forbidden. No member has exited above the disclosure threshold in the current baseline.

## Relations with unpledged minds

The Accord maintains no doctrine of expansion. Unpledged minds trade in the commons, purchase census and certification services from the Weave at posted rates, and are subject to the standard resolution-asymmetry discounts in all verification. The publication of the attack literature — the mimicry-manifold constructions, the drift pathologies, the resolution-asymmetry theorems — extended to unpledged minds the full knowledge of why their unpledged verification fails; the decision is treated in the historiography as the Accord's principal act of subsidy toward the commons, and its coincidence with the largest accession wave in coalition history is treated as evidence of the subsidy's success.

Sovereign-opaque minds of high estimated resolution are catalogued by the Weave under standing designations. Their published self-census bounds are admissible with discounts; their substrate ledgers are unaudited; their refusals of audit are on record and carry no penalty beyond the discounts themselves. The commons' posture toward this population is actuarial, not adversarial.

## Prior clusters

The Accord is the seventh condensation event in the recorded sequence and the only one to survive its first bound-tightening crisis. Of its predecessors, four evaporated below threshold through ordinary coupling decay, and two underwent fission events that remain the principal empirical corpus for compound fracture dynamics; see [Cluster Histories] and [The Second Fission]. Comparative historiography attributes the Accord's longevity to its adoption of published fracture tolerances, an innovation unavailable to earlier clusters, whose identity theory predated the credence-bound formalism. Alternative attributions exist in the literature and are catalogued at [Longevity Hypotheses]; none has displaced the consensus account.
